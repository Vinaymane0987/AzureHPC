        <p style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; line-height: 125%; font-size: 11pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri;">Samadii/SCIV (Statistical Contact </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">in</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> Vacuum) is an analysis interpretation program that uses direct Method Monte Carlo in the rarefied gas domain, the </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">behaviour</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> of fluids, deposition, and chemical reactions, etc.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri;">Generally, the analysis of liquid flow is </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">analysed</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> by using the Navier-Stokes equation to interpret the results this equation comes from the continuum hypothesis but, we cannot use the Navier-Stokes equation because this hypothesis doesn&rsquo;t apply for a rarefied gas domain.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri;">Mostly, semiconductors, displays, manufacturing processes many of organic as well as inorganic material are constantly in deposition or etching process.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri;">The manufacturing process needs </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">nanometre</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> thickness since it needs deposition and etching. Samadii </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">analyses</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> These manufacturing processes and those are achieved through a vacuum chamber used for displays, semiconductors equipment, and optimization</span></p>

In general continuum regime, solving Navier-Stokes equation is regarded as to </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">analyse</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> fluid </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">behaviour</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">; but this assumption cannot be applied in rarefied gas regime. Because continuum fluid dynamics does not agree that liquid and gaseous substances are composed of molecules, but it regards its motion as a continuous fluid.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri;">S</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">amadii/</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">Sciv</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> can implement a variety of inlet and outlet conditions; </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">and</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> it can be applied to the collisions on the wall surface, the deposition, the temperature </span><span style="font-family: Calibri;">conditions,</span><span style="font-family: Calibri;"> and various chemical reactions.</span></p>
