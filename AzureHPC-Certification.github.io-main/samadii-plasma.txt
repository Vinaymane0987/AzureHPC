        <p style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; line-height: 125%; font-size: 11pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri;">Samadii Plasma </span><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">particle-based solution is packed with improved performance. In Samadii-Plasma it is possible to analyse the plasma process through</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">ion &amp; electron particles.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">Samadii-Plasma provides high-performance and advanced plasma physics simulation.</span> <span style="font-family: Calibri;">Samadii-Plasma used </span><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">For Analysis of ion and electron </span><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">behaviour</span><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;"> in an electromagnetic field.</span></p>
        <p style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; line-height: 125%; font-size: 11pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">Plasma is a gaseous mixture of negatively charged electrons and highly charged ions created by electro-magnetic field and it is applied as essential core technology for film deposition, etching and surface functionalization in manufacturing of value-added products including devices, components and equipment&rsquo;s for prospective industries including semiconductor, flat panel displays, digital electronics etc.</span></p>
        <p style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; line-height: 125%; font-size: 11pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">Plasma is a complex phenomenon that the movement of particles and ions mutually influences electromagnetic field. In plasma states, the temperature of gas particles can be measured very different because the neutral, ions, electrons have different mobility in electromagnetic fields. Also, the collisions between the particles occurs constantly which cause a plasma reaction (ionization, excitation, etc) as well as chemical reaction.</span></p>
        <p style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; line-height: 125%; font-size: 11pt;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; color: #1d2129; background-color: #ffffff;">Calculation of the plasma with direct simulation based on particle-based method requires large amount of computational time. Therefore, the grid-based plasma flow analysis regards that the nature of the ions and electrons as two or more fluids. But basically, plasma is composed of particles, and it is essentially based on the analysis of particle method to correctly interpret the plasma reaction due to the collisions between these particles. More advanced plasma analysis is available due to high-speed electromagnetic field analysis capabilities and particle-based gas analysis solution of samadii/plasma based on the GPU.</span></p>
